Projects


AUDIONET

A cross-platform text-to-speech application that allows users to organize and listen to their digital reading naturally.

Audionet was envisoned to provide a new aural experience in content consumption where users could organise and listen to digital texts by leveraging recent improvements in natural language processing and speech technology (commonly referred to as text-to-speech or TTS). We aimed at allowing our users to listen to natural, human-like voices while consuming content through a service which would enable the users consume more contant in a typical day of their life, thereby helping them create more time.

How Does Audionet Help You Create More Time ?





Journey . . .

Understanding how the external (Social, Economic and Technological) factors weigh into creating an opportunity gap for a text-to-speech applicaiton like Audionet.
Validating our hypotheisis and understanding customer needs better along with their pain points in order to rebundle and tweak our offerings.
Analysing the desirability, feasibility and viability of the service by mapping the flow of value between different stakeholders
Forming an initial hypothesis and a framework based on PESTLE, SET and POG analysis to identify a customer segment along with their needs.
Understanding how Audionet is uniquely positioned with respect to its competitors and what are the key aspects of its differentialted value proposition that it provides its customers.
Revising the value flow as a result of extensive customer research and pivot away from students and visually impaired to focusing only on working professionals.

Pivot

"Pivoting is not the end of the disruption process, but the beginning of the next leg of your journey." - Jay Samit
With a refined value proposition backed by the customer research, we reinforced and highlighted some critical components of the Minumum Viable Product (MVP) for our text-to-speech service by articulating it in the form of a Business Model Canvas.
